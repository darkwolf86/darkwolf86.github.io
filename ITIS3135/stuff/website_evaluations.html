Website Evaluations

Good Website Bad Website

Good Website – BBC News

The BBC’s news portal demonstrates exemplary use of contrast, hierarchy, and accessibility. Its white‑on‑black color scheme provides excellent readability, while semantic markup (H1–H6) guides screen readers.

Link to the site

BBC News – Live

CRAP Principles That Work Well

  • Contrast: High contrast between text and background.
  • Relevance: Content is tailored to the news audience.
  • Aesthetics: Clean, uncluttered layout with ample whitespace.
  • Perceptibility: Images have alt text; headings are semantic.

Bad Website – Yale Art & Architecture

While rich in imagery, this site suffers from low contrast, missing alt text, and a cluttered layout that confuses users.

Link to the site

Yale Art & Architecture

CRAP Principles That Fail

  • Contrast: Light text on a dark background with no contrast checks.
  • Relevance: Navigation menus are nested and not labelled, confusing the user’s sense of context.
  • Aesthetics: Overuse of large images, no whitespace, and a heavy color palette create visual noise.
  • Perceptibility: Many images lack alt attributes; icons are not accessible to screen readers.

*If I were redesigning it, I’d start with a high‑contrast color scheme, add descriptive alt text, and simplify the navigation into a single horizontal bar. A consistent grid would keep the page from feeling chaotic.*
